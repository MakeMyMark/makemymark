Section 5

No Guarantee of Registration

Make My Mark does not guarantee that your trademark application will be approved or that your mark will be registered with the USPTO. Trademark registration is subject to the discretion of USPTO examining attorneys and is governed by federal trademark law.

Common reasons for denial include, but are not limited to:

  • Likelihood of confusion with an existing registered mark
  • The mark being merely descriptive of the goods or services
  • The mark being generic
  • Failure to establish use in commerce
  • Geographic descriptiveness
  • Primarily merely a surname

Make My Mark will conduct a preliminary clearance search prior to filing and will advise you of any identified risks. However, a clearance search is not exhaustive and cannot identify all potential conflicts or grounds for refusal. Filing a trademark application always carries inherent risk of denial, and the decision to proceed is ultimately yours.

By submitting payment and proceeding with filing, you acknowledge and accept that trademark registration is not guaranteed, and that denial by the USPTO does not constitute negligence or error on the part of Make My Mark or Graves Law Firm, PLLC, except where caused by a demonstrable error in the preparation or filing of your application.
