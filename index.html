Questions

Everything you
need to know.

What exactly is a trademark? +
A trademark is a word, phrase, symbol, or design that identifies and distinguishes the source of your goods or services. Registering with the USPTO gives you nationwide protection and the right to use the ® symbol.
Do I need a trademark if I have an LLC? +
Yes. An LLC only protects your business name within your state. A federal trademark protects your brand name, logo, and slogan across all 50 states and gives you priority rights if someone else tries to use your mark.
How long does the process take? +
We file within 48 hours of your order. The USPTO typically takes 8–12 months to fully process an application. Once filed, you can use ™ immediately — and ® upon registration.
What are the USPTO fees exactly? +
The USPTO currently charges $350 per class for TEAS applications. Additional surcharges apply for custom descriptions ($200/class), descriptions over 1,000 characters ($200 per extra 1,000), and missing information ($100/class). We help you avoid every surcharge.
Why not just use a $49 filing service? +
Those services charge $28–$49 as their fee — but you still pay the $350 USPTO fee on top. More importantly, no licensed attorney reviews your application. When the USPTO sends an Office Action (which happens frequently), you're on your own — or paying extra. With us, an attorney is on your file from day one.
What is an Office Action? +
An Office Action is a letter from the USPTO raising questions or objections about your application. Missing the response deadline abandons your application. Our Standard plan includes one response; Plus and above include unlimited responses through registration.
Can I trademark a logo or slogan? +
Absolutely. You can trademark a name, logo, slogan, or combination mark. Simply indicate the type of mark in the intake form and upload your logo file if applicable.
What is trademark monitoring? +
After registration, others can file marks that conflict with yours. Our Premium plan includes 12 months of monitoring — we alert you when a potentially conflicting application is filed so you can act quickly.
What happens if my trademark is denied? +
If the USPTO ultimately denies your application after all available Office Action responses are exhausted, you'll receive a refund of your attorney service fee minus $250 — which covers the work already performed preparing, filing, and responding on your behalf. USPTO government filing fees ($350/class) are non-refundable by federal law. Premium plan clients receive a full attorney fee refund if their mark is ultimately denied. See our Terms & Conditions for full details.
What if I change my mind before filing? +
If you cancel before your application is filed, you'll receive a full refund minus a $100 non-refundable review fee that covers attorney time spent on your preliminary clearance search and registrability assessment. If our attorney advises against filing and you choose not to proceed, the same policy applies.
